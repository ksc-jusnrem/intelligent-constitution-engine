# Indian Councils Act 1909 (Morley–Minto Reforms)

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1909-05-25  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

Introduced direct election to legislative councils — and, decisively for the future Pakistan, separate electorates under which Muslims voted for Muslim representatives. The demand of the Simla Deputation (1906) and the newly founded All-India Muslim League thus entered constitutional law, recognising Muslims as a distinct political community.

## Key provisions and effects

- Separate electorates and reserved seats for Muslims
- Elected non-official majorities in provincial councils
- First Indian admitted to the Viceroy's Executive Council (S.P. Sinha)
- Members could move resolutions and ask supplementary questions

## How it ended

Absorbed into the Government of India Act 1919; separate electorates persisted to 1947 and shaped the two-nation constitutional argument.
