# Asma Jilani v. Government of the Punjab

**Era:** Transition — From Martial Law to the 1973 Constitution (1969–1973)  
**Date:** 1972-04  
**Type:** case

## Dossier

The Supreme Court overruled Dosso: Kelsen's doctrine was rejected, Yahya Khan was declared a usurper, and the 1969 martial law was held illegal. The doctrine of necessity survived only in narrow, condonation form — the high-water mark of judicial resistance to extra-constitutional rule.

## Key provisions and effects

- Dosso overruled; revolutionary-legality doctrine rejected
- Yahya's regime branded usurpation; its acts subject to condonation, not validation
- Reasserted that sovereignty belongs to the people under law

## How it ended

Its principles were strained by later validations (Nusrat Bhutto 1977, Zafar Ali Shah 2000) but never formally abandoned.
