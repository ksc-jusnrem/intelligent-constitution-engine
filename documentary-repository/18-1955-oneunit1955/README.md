# Establishment of West Pakistan Act 1955 (One Unit)

**Era:** Dominion of Pakistan — the Interim Framework (1947–1956)  
**Date:** 1955-09-30  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

Merged Punjab, Sindh, NWFP, Balochistan and the states of the western wing into a single province of West Pakistan (effective 14 October 1955), to create parity between one eastern and one western unit ahead of the new constitution.

## Key provisions and effects

- One province for the entire western wing; East Bengal became East Pakistan
- Parity principle: equal representation for the two wings regardless of population
- Second Constituent Assembly (convened summer 1955) enacted the scheme

## How it ended

One Unit was dissolved by Yahya Khan's regime with effect from 1 July 1970, restoring the four provinces.
