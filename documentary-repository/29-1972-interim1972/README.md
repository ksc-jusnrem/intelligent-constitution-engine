# Interim Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1972

**Era:** Transition — From Martial Law to the 1973 Constitution (1969–1973)  
**Date:** 1972-04-21  
**Type:** constitution

## Dossier

Adopted by the National Assembly to end martial law (lifted the same day) and govern while the permanent constitution was drafted. A presidential interim scheme that restored fundamental rights and the Objectives Resolution preamble.

## Key provisions and effects

- Martial law lifted 21 April 1972; President Bhutto continued as chief executive
- Fundamental rights restored and justiciable
- National Assembly doubled as constituent body, its constitutional committee (under Mahmud Ali Kasuri, then agreed by all parliamentary parties in the constitutional accord of October 1972) drafting the permanent text

## How it ended

Replaced by the permanent Constitution: passed by the Assembly 10 April 1973, assented 12 April 1973, in force 14 August 1973.
