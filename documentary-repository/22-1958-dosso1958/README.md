# The State v. Dosso

**Era:** The 1956 Constitution and the First Martial Law (1956–1962)  
**Date:** 1958-10  
**Type:** case

## Dossier

Within weeks of the coup the Supreme Court, again under Chief Justice Munir, upheld the new order: relying on Hans Kelsen's theory that a successful revolution creates its own legality, it held that the 1958 abrogation, having succeeded, was a valid source of law.

## Key provisions and effects

- Kelsenian 'revolutionary legality' adopted into Pakistani jurisprudence
- Fundamental rights held non-enforceable after abrogation
- Gave judicial cover to the first military regime

## How it ended

Expressly overruled in Asma Jilani v. Government of Punjab (1972), which branded such takeovers usurpation.
