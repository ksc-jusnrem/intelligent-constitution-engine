# The 1971 Crisis and the Secession of East Pakistan

**Era:** Transition — From Martial Law to the 1973 Constitution (1969–1973)  
**Date:** 1971-12-16  
**Type:** milestone

## Dossier

The postponement of the Assembly, the crackdown of 25 March 1971 and a nine-month war ended with the surrender at Dacca on 16 December 1971. East Pakistan became Bangladesh; the constitutional problem of two wings — parity, language, autonomy — that had shaped every framework since 1947 ended in rupture. Yahya Khan resigned on 20 December 1971, handing power to Zulfikar Ali Bhutto as President and the first civilian Chief Martial Law Administrator.

## Key provisions and effects

- Federation reduced to its western wing
- Bhutto: civilian President and CMLA from 20 December 1971
- Constitution-making resumed with the National Assembly elected in 1970 (West-wing members)

## How it ended

Set the stage for the Interim Constitution of 1972 and the permanent constitution of 1973.
