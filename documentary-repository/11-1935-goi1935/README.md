# Government of India Act 1935

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1935-08-02  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

The longest statute Parliament had ever passed (over 300 sections and 10 schedules) and the direct ancestor of both Pakistani and Indian constitutional law. Adapted in 1947, it served as Pakistan's working constitution for nearly nine years.

## Key provisions and effects

- Provincial autonomy replaced dyarchy — fully responsible ministries after the 1937 elections
- Scheme for an All-India Federation of provinces and princely states (never realised — the states withheld accession)
- Dyarchy moved to the centre; Governor-General's discretionary powers and 'safeguards' retained
- Federal Court established (1937) — predecessor of the Federal Court of Pakistan
- Detailed legislative lists (federal, provincial, concurrent) — a template every Pakistani constitution kept
- Burma separated; Sindh and Orissa created as provinces — Sindh's separation a long-standing Muslim demand

## How it ended

Continued in force, as adapted, as the interim constitution of Pakistan (1947–1956) and of India (1947–1950).
