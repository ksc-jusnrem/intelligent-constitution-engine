# Government of India Act 1858

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1858-08-02  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

The first Government of India Act: it abolished the East India Company and the Board of Control and transferred the government of India directly to the British Crown, in response to the uprising of 1857.

## Key provisions and effects

- Secretary of State for India, answerable to Parliament, advised by a 15-member Council of India
- Governor-General became also the Viceroy, the Crown's direct representative
- All Company territories, forces, debts and treaties vested in the Crown
- Queen Victoria's Proclamation (1 November 1858) promised non-interference in religion and equal protection of law

## How it ended

Remained the foundation of British-Indian government, layered over by the Councils Acts and the 1919 and 1935 Acts, until repealed for the new dominions by the Indian Independence Act 1947.
