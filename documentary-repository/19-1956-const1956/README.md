# Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1956

**Era:** The 1956 Constitution and the First Martial Law (1956–1962)  
**Date:** 1956-03-23  
**Type:** constitution

## Dossier

Passed by the second Constituent Assembly on 29 February 1956 and commenced on 23 March (the Lahore Resolution anniversary). A parliamentary federation of two units in 234 articles — the first constitution anywhere to name the state an Islamic Republic.

## Key provisions and effects

- Islamic Republic; Objectives Resolution as preamble
- Parliamentary system: President (must be Muslim) as head of state, Prime Minister responsible to a unicameral National Assembly
- Parity: 150 seats split equally between East and West Pakistan
- Urdu and Bengali both national languages
- Repugnancy clause: no law contrary to the Quran and Sunnah; Islamic research institutions
- Fundamental rights, justiciable in superior courts; Supreme Court replacing the Federal Court

## How it ended

Abrogated, never having seen a general election, by proclamation of President Iskander Mirza on 7 October 1958.
