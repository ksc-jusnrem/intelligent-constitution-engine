# Regulating Act 1773

**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)  
**Date:** 1773  
**Type:** precursor

## Dossier

Parliament's first direct intervention in the East India Company's governance of Indian territories, prompted by the Company's misrule and near-bankruptcy after the Bengal famine.

## Key provisions and effects

- Created the office of Governor-General of Bengal (Warren Hastings) with a Council of four
- Subordinated the presidencies of Madras and Bombay to Bengal
- Established the Supreme Court at Calcutta (1774) — first Crown court on Indian soil

## How it ended

Superseded progressively by Pitt's India Act 1784.
