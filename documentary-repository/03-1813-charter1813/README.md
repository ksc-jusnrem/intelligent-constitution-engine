# Charter Act 1813

**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)  
**Date:** 1813  
**Type:** precursor

## Dossier

Renewed the Company's charter while breaking its commercial monopoly and, for the first time, expressly asserting the 'undoubted sovereignty of the Crown' over the Company's Indian territories.

## Key provisions and effects

- Ended the trade monopoly except in tea and trade with China
- Asserted Crown sovereignty over Company territories
- First public allocation for education of Indian subjects

## How it ended

Renewed and deepened by the Charter Act 1833.
