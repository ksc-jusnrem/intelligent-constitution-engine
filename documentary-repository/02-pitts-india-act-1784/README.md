# Pitt's India Act 1784
**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)
**Date:** 13 August 1784 | **Citation:** 24 Geo. III Sess. 2 c. 25 | **Type:** precursor

## Summary
Created the 'double government' system: a Crown Board of Control with supreme authority over India's political and military affairs, alongside the Company which retained commercial and patronage functions. This architecture governed India for seventy-four years.

## Key Provisions
- Board of Control: six members (two Cabinet ministers + four Privy Councillors)
- Board's superintendence over all political, military, and revenue affairs of India
- Secret Committee of three Directors for urgent dispatches
- Governor-General appointed by Crown on recommendation of Directors
- Prohibition on offensive wars without Board consent
- Corrupt acts punishable in English courts
