# Constitution of the Republic of Pakistan, 1962

**Era:** The 1962 Constitution and the Second Martial Law (1962–1969)  
**Date:** 1962-06-08  
**Type:** constitution

## Dossier

Promulgated by President Ayub Khan on 1 March 1962 — not adopted by any constituent assembly — and brought into force on 8 June 1962, ending martial law. A presidential constitution of 250 articles built on the Basic Democracies system; the Constitution Commission (Justice Shahabuddin, 1960–61) had recommended otherwise on key points and was overridden.

## Key provisions and effects

- Presidential system: all executive power in the President; ministers his appointees, not MPs
- Indirect elections: an electoral college of 80,000 (later 120,000) Basic Democrats chose President and assemblies
- Unicameral National Assembly with limited financial powers; One Unit and parity retained
- Initially 'Republic of Pakistan' with non-justiciable Principles of Law-making — the First Amendment (1963, effective January 1964) restored the name Islamic Republic and made fundamental rights justiciable
- Advisory Council of Islamic Ideology and Islamic Research Institute established

## How it ended

Abrogated on 25 March 1969 when Ayub Khan handed power to the army rather than to the Speaker, as his own constitution required.
