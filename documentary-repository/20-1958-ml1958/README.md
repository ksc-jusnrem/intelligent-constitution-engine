# First Martial Law — Abrogation of the 1956 Constitution

**Era:** The 1956 Constitution and the First Martial Law (1956–1962)  
**Date:** 1958-10-07  
**Type:** martial

## Dossier

President Iskander Mirza abrogated the constitution, dissolved the national and provincial assemblies, dismissed the cabinet, banned political parties and appointed the army chief, General Muhammad Ayub Khan, Chief Martial Law Administrator. Twenty days later, on 27 October 1958, Ayub deposed Mirza himself and assumed the presidency.

## Key provisions and effects

- Constitution abrogated; assemblies dissolved; parties banned
- Country placed under martial law for the first time
- Ayub Khan: CMLA, then President from 27 October 1958

## How it ended

Martial law lifted on 8 June 1962, when Ayub's own constitution came into force.
