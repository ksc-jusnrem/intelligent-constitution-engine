# Regulating Act 1773
**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)
**Date:** 10 June 1773 | **Citation:** 13 Geo. III c. 63 | **Type:** precursor

## Summary
The first Parliamentary act to regulate the East India Company's governance of Bengal. It introduced the principle that the Company's territorial and political functions were subject to Parliamentary oversight.

## Key Provisions
- Governor-General of Bengal with Council of Four (majority vote; casting vote to Governor-General)
- Supreme Court at Calcutta — three Crown-appointed judges
- Presidencies of Bombay and Madras subordinated to Bengal in matters of war and peace
- Prohibition on Company servants accepting presents or engaging in private trade
- Company required to lay dispatches before the Secretary of State
