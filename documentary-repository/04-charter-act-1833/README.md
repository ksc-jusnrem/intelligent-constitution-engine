# Charter Act 1833 (Government of India Act 1833)
**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)
**Date:** 28 August 1833 | **Citation:** 3 & 4 Will. IV c. 85 | **Type:** precursor

## Summary
Completed the transformation of the East India Company from a trading corporation into an imperial government. By abolishing the China trade monopoly (the last commercial privilege) and vesting all law-making power in a single central legislature, it created the constitutional skeleton of British India as it would exist until 1858.

## Key Provisions
- Governor-General of India (not Bengal) — supreme legislative and executive authority
- Company ceases all commercial activity
- Section 87 — non-discrimination clause in public employment
- Indian Law Commission — Thomas Macaulay as first Law Member
- Central Legislative Council with a law member
- Governor-General's Council to have four members
