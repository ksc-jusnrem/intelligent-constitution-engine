# Lahore Resolution

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1940-03-23  
**Type:** milestone

## Dossier

The All-India Muslim League, meeting at Lahore under Jinnah, resolved that areas where Muslims were a majority 'should be grouped to constitute Independent States' — the constitutional demand from which Pakistan was created. 23 March was later chosen to commence the 1956 Constitution, and remains Republic Day.

## Key provisions and effects

- Demanded independent states in Muslim-majority zones of the north-west and east
- Rejected a single all-India federation under the 1935 Act scheme
- Became the Muslim League's settled constitutional position in the Cabinet Mission negotiations (1946) and the partition settlement (3 June Plan, 1947)

## How it ended

Fulfilled by the Indian Independence Act 1947.
