# Government of India Act 1919 (Montagu–Chelmsford Reforms)

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1919-12-23  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

Implemented the Montagu Declaration (1917) promising 'progressive realisation of responsible government'. Its signature device was dyarchy: provincial subjects split between ministers answerable to elected legislators and officials answerable to the Governor.

## Key provisions and effects

- Dyarchy in provinces: 'transferred' subjects (education, health, local government) under Indian ministers; 'reserved' subjects (police, revenue, justice) under the Governor
- Bicameral central legislature: Council of State and Legislative Assembly
- Separate electorates extended to Sikhs and others
- Statutory review after ten years — producing the Simon Commission and the Round Table Conferences

## How it ended

Replaced by the Government of India Act 1935 after the dyarchy experiment was judged unworkable.
