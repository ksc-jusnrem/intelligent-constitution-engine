# Charter Act 1853
**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)
**Date:** 20 August 1853 | **Citation:** 16 & 17 Vict. c. 95 | **Type:** precursor

## Summary
The last act of the Company era. By leaving the charter open-ended and opening the Civil Service to competitive examination, it created the conditions for the Company's own irrelevance. Four years later the Mutiny of 1857 ended the Company forever.

## Key Provisions
- Charter renewed indefinitely — Parliament may revoke at any time
- Competitive examinations for the Indian Civil Service — open to all
- Legislative Council enlarged and formally separated from Executive Council
- Six additional Legislative Councillors from the major provinces
- Standing Parliamentary committee on Indian affairs
