# Indian Independence Act 1947

**Era:** Dominion of Pakistan — the Interim Framework (1947–1956)  
**Date:** 1947-07-18  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

Parliament's act of severance: from 15 August 1947 (Pakistan observed 14 August), two independent Dominions, each with a sovereign Constituent Assembly empowered both to legislate and to frame a constitution.

## Key provisions and effects

- Created the Dominions of Pakistan and India and ended British responsibility
- Crown's paramountcy over the princely states lapsed — states free to accede
- Each Constituent Assembly made sovereign; no British act to extend automatically
- Government of India Act 1935 to continue, as adapted, until each dominion framed its own constitution

## How it ended

Spent its force as Pakistan's constitutional instruments replaced it; formally superseded by the 1956 Constitution.
