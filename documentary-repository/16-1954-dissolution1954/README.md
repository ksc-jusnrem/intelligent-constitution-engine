# Dissolution of the first Constituent Assembly

**Era:** Dominion of Pakistan — the Interim Framework (1947–1956)  
**Date:** 1954-10-24  
**Type:** martial

## Dossier

Days after the Assembly moved to strip the Governor-General of the power to dismiss ministries, Governor-General Ghulam Muhammad declared a state of emergency and dissolved it — seven years of drafting (including the agreed Basic Principles Committee report) extinguished by executive decree.

## Key provisions and effects

- Assembly dissolved on the claim that it had 'lost the confidence of the people'
- Draft constitution then near completion was abandoned
- Set up the confrontation tested in the Tamizuddin litigation

## How it ended

Validated, in effect, by the Federal Court in 1955 — entrenching executive supremacy over elected assemblies.
