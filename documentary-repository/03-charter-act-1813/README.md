# Charter Act 1813
**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)
**Date:** 21 July 1813 | **Citation:** 53 Geo. III c. 155 | **Type:** precursor

## Summary
The first Charter Act to break the Company's trading monopoly over India (China trade retained until 1833). It established the state's responsibility for education and opened India to missionary activity — two forces that profoundly reshaped Indian society.

## Key Provisions
- Indian trade thrown open; China and tea monopoly retained
- Crown's sovereignty over British possessions in India explicitly affirmed
- Rs. 1 lakh per annum reserved for education and sciences
- Missionaries licensed to enter India
- Governor-General in Council empowered to levy customs duties
