# Charter Act 1833 (Government of India Act 1833)

**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)  
**Date:** 1833  
**Type:** precursor

## Dossier

Turned the Company into a purely administrative trustee of the Crown and centralised all law-making in a single authority for the whole of India.

## Key provisions and effects

- Governor-General of Bengal became Governor-General of India (Lord William Bentinck first)
- All legislative power centralised in the Governor-General-in-Council
- Law Member added to Council (Macaulay) — beginning of professional codification
- Company's commercial functions ended entirely

## How it ended

Modified by the Charter Act 1853; structure swept away in 1858.
