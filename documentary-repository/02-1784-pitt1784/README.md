# Pitt's India Act 1784

**Era:** Company Rule — Precursor Statutes (1773–1853)  
**Date:** 1784  
**Type:** precursor

## Dossier

Established 'dual control': the Company retained commerce and patronage, but a Crown-appointed Board of Control supervised all civil, military and revenue affairs — the constitutional germ of direct Crown rule.

## Key provisions and effects

- Board of Control in London over Company's political functions
- Distinguished commercial from political functions of the Company
- Governor-General's Council reduced and subordinated to London

## How it ended

The dual-control system lasted until both Company and Board were abolished in 1858.
