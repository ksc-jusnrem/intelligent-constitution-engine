# Indian Councils Act 1861

**Era:** Crown Rule — Constitutional Frameworks of British India (1858–1947)  
**Date:** 1861-08-01  
**Type:** statute

## Dossier

Re-admitted Indians to the legislative process for the first time since 1857 and decentralised legislation back to the provinces.

## Key provisions and effects

- Non-official members (including Indians) nominated to the Governor-General's Legislative Council
- Legislative powers restored to Bombay and Madras; new councils for other provinces authorised
- Portfolio system introduced (Canning) — embryo of cabinet government
- Viceroy empowered to issue ordinances — a power every successor state retained

## How it ended

Expanded by the Indian Councils Act 1892.
