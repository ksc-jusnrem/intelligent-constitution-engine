(1) The President shall constitute a National Economic Council consisting of the Prime Minister, who shall be the Chairman of the National Economic Council, the Chief Ministers and one member from each Province to be nominated by the Chief Minister and four other members as the Prime Minister may nominate from time to time.

(2) The National Economic Council shall review the overall economic condition of the country and shall, for advising the Federal Government and the Provincial Governments, formulate plans in respect of financial, commercial, social and economic policies and in formulating such plans, it shall be guided by the Principles of Policy set out in Chapter 2 of Part II.

(3) The National Economic Council shall meet at least twice in a year and the quorum for a meeting of the National Economic Council shall be one-half of the total membership.

(4) The National Economic Council shall be constituted within thirty days of the Prime Minister taking oath of office.

(5) The National Economic Council shall be responsible to the Parliament and shall, in each year, submit to both Houses of Parliament an annual report on the state of the economy and shall ensure that consultations for economic policy making have been conducted with all stakeholders.
