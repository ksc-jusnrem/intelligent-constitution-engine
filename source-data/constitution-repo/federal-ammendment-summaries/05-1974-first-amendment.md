The most immediate impact was a "legal closing of the door" on the past. By officially removing East Pakistan from the Constitution, the state finally aligned its supreme law with the reality of its new borders. It was a somber but necessary administrative step to stabilize the identity of the "new" Pakistan.

However, for the average citizen and political worker, the impact was a bit more restrictive. The amendment gave the government a powerful legal tool to ban political parties it deemed a threat to national integrity. While the Supreme Court was kept as a "safety valve" to make the final call, the balance of power shifted toward the executive branch. Additionally, by restricting the High Court's jurisdiction over military and civil service matters, the amendment created "specialized lanes" for legal disputes, making it harder for those in the service of Pakistan to seek traditional judicial relief in standard courts.
